Privacy Policy

We Print the Past — Never Your Data.

No accounts. No tracking. No analytics. Everything the app saves stays on your device.

Last Updated · 18 June 2026

FlashBack does not collect any of your data. There are no accounts, no logins, no tracking, no ads, and no analytics. Everything the app saves stays on your device. The only time FlashBack uses the internet is to fetch historical facts and images from Wikipedia — and even then, nothing about you is sent.

Section One

Who We Are

FlashBack is a daily history app that shows you six personalized historical stories tied to today's date, written in a newspaper style. It is made by an independent individual developer based in Poland, in the European Union. For the EU's GDPR, that developer is the "data controller" — but as explained below, FlashBack does not collect or process any personal data, so in practice there is nothing about you for anyone to hold.

Section Two

What We Collect: Nothing

None. FlashBack has no user accounts and no sign-in. We do not collect, receive, store on a server, sell, share, or transmit any personal data. We do not collect your name, email, location, contacts, advertising identifiers, or any usage analytics.

Section Three

What Is Stored on Your Device

So the app can work and remember your choices, FlashBack saves a few things locally on your device only. This information never leaves your device, and we have no access to it:

  • The interest categories you pick, and the priority order you arrange them in.
  • Your reading streak and history (which days you opened an edition, and how many editions you've read).
  • Your notification preference (on or off).
  • A cached copy of your recent daily editions, so the app opens instantly.

You can erase all of this at any time with Reset FlashBack in Settings, or by deleting the app.

Section Four

The Internet Connection (Wikipedia)

To show real, accurate history, FlashBack makes anonymous requests to Wikipedia's public REST API for facts and images. The only thing sent is the calendar date (today's date), used to look up "on this day" events. We do not send your identity, your interests, any device identifier, or any personal information. These requests are served by the Wikimedia Foundation under its own privacy policy: foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Policy:Privacy_policy.

Section Five

On-Device Artificial Intelligence

FlashBack rewrites the historical facts into newspaper-style stories using Apple's on-device Foundation Models (Apple Intelligence). All AI processing happens entirely on your device. No data — not the facts, not your interests, not the generated stories — is ever sent to us or any external server for AI processing.

Section Six

Notifications

FlashBack can show an optional daily reminder around 9:30 AM. This is a local notification scheduled on your device — no push server, no backend. You can turn it on or off in Settings or in the iOS Settings app. If you never turn it on, no notifications are scheduled.

Section Seven

Third Parties

None. No third-party analytics, advertising, crash-reporting, or tracking software. The only outside service contacted is Wikipedia (Section Four).

Section Eight

Children's Privacy

FlashBack collects no data from anyone, including children. It has no accounts and no data collection, and is suitable for a general audience.

Section Nine

Your Privacy Rights (GDPR & Others)

Because FlashBack does not collect or process any personal data, there is no personal data for us to look up, correct, export, or delete. Everything the app stores lives on your own device and stays fully under your control — remove it all with Reset FlashBack or by deleting the app. If you have privacy questions, contact the developer (Section Eleven). If you are in the EU, you may also complain to your local data protection authority; in Poland that is the President of the Personal Data Protection Office (UODO).

Section Ten

Data Retention

We keep no data, because we collect none. Anything stored locally on your device remains until you reset or delete the app.
